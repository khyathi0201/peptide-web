Antimicrobial Peptide Prediction Tool

About the Tool

This web-based Antimicrobial Peptide (AMP) Prediction Tool is designed to identify and evaluate peptide sequences for their antimicrobial potential using a hybrid machine learning framework. The system integrates multiple predictive models and biologically relevant rules to classify peptides based on their antimicrobial activity, toxicity, and functional effectiveness.

Antimicrobial peptides are short bioactive molecules that play a crucial role in innate immunity by inhibiting the growth of bacteria, fungi, and viruses. Due to increasing antimicrobial resistance, computational prediction of AMPs has become an important area of research in bioinformatics and drug discovery.


Prediction Objectives:
  • Identify whether a peptide is antimicrobial (AMP) or non-antimicrobial
  • Evaluate the toxicity profile of the peptide
  • Predict biological activity based on Minimum Inhibitory Concentration (MIC)
  • Provide a final classification integrating all predictions

Machine Learning Framework:
  • Support Vector Machine (SVM): Used for AMP classification based on high-dimensional sequence features (534 features)
  • Random Forest: Used for toxicity prediction using physicochemical properties
  • XGBoost: Used for activity prediction based on MIC-derived labels

Feature Engineering:

The model uses a comprehensive set of sequence-derived features including:

  • Amino Acid Composition (AAC)
  • Dipeptide Composition (ASDC)
  • Grouped Amino Acid Composition (GAAC)
  • Physicochemical descriptors such as hydrophobicity, charge, and molecular weight
  • Sequence pattern and structural tendency features

Prediction Thresholds:
  • AMP classification threshold: 0.50
  • Toxicity threshold: 0.45
  • Activity threshold: 0.40
  • Highly active threshold: 0.70

Biological Constraint:

A rule-based biological filter is applied where peptides with net charge ≤ 0 are classified as non-antimicrobial. This is based on the established observation that most antimicrobial peptides are positively charged and interact with negatively charged microbial membranes.


Final Classification Categories:
  • Safe Antimicrobial Peptide
  • Toxic Antimicrobial Peptide
  • Inactive Antimicrobial Peptide
  • Non-Antimicrobial Peptide

Dataset and Training:

The models are trained using curated peptide datasets containing experimentally validated antimicrobial activity, toxicity, and MIC values. Cross-validation techniques are employed to ensure robustness and generalization of the models.


Applications:
  • Drug discovery and peptide design
  • Screening of novel antimicrobial candidates
  • Bioinformatics research and education
  • Understanding structure-function relationships in peptides

Limitations:

The predictions are generated using machine learning models trained on curated datasets and may not fully capture all biological complexities. While the models demonstrate high predictive performance (approximately 85–90% accuracy), the results are probabilistic in nature and should be validated experimentally for critical applications.
